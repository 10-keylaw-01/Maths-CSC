Departmental Activities

Explore our academic, research, and extra-curricular activities

Our Activities

The Department of Mathematics and Computer Science organizes various activities throughout the academic year to enrich students' learning experience, promote research, and foster collaboration with industry partners. These activities are designed to complement classroom learning, develop practical skills, and provide networking opportunities.

Annual Coding Competition

Our annual coding competition is a highlight of the department's calendar. Students form teams to solve challenging programming problems within a limited time frame. The competition tests algorithmic thinking, problem-solving skills, and teamwork.

Winners receive prizes sponsored by leading technology companies, and top performers are often offered internship opportunities. The competition is open to all students enrolled in the department, from first-year undergraduates to postgraduate researchers.

Previous competition themes have included sustainable technology solutions, artificial intelligence applications, and mobile app development.

When: October (Fall Semester)

Where: Main Computer Laboratory

Registration: Opens one month before the event

Research Symposium

The departmental Research Symposium provides a platform for students and faculty to present their research findings, exchange ideas, and initiate collaborations. The symposium features oral presentations, poster sessions, and keynote speeches from distinguished researchers in mathematics and computer science.

This event exposes undergraduate students to advanced research topics and methodologies, inspiring them to pursue research interests. For postgraduate students and faculty, it offers an opportunity to receive feedback on ongoing projects and establish connections with researchers working in similar areas.

Selected papers from the symposium are published in the department's annual research journal, providing valuable publication experience for early-career researchers.

When: April (Spring Semester)

Where: Department Conference Hall

Submission Deadline: February 15

Industry Connect Program

The Industry Connect Program bridges the gap between academic learning and industry requirements. Throughout the year, we invite professionals from various technology companies and research institutions to interact with our students through guest lectures, workshops, and networking sessions.

These events provide students with insights into current industry trends, employment opportunities, and the practical applications of mathematical and computational concepts. They also help students develop professional networks and identify potential career paths.

The program includes an annual career fair where students can meet recruiters from leading companies and explore internship and job opportunities.

When: Throughout the academic year

Where: Various locations on campus

Format: Guest lectures, workshops, career fair

Mathematics and Computer Science Club

The student-run Mathematics and Computer Science Club organizes weekly meetings, study groups, and social events to foster a sense of community within the department. The club provides a relaxed environment for students to discuss academic concepts, work on collaborative projects, and develop friendships with peers sharing similar interests.

Activities include coding sessions, mathematics problem-solving competitions, game development workshops, and recreational mathematics explorations. The club also arranges field trips to technology conferences and mathematics exhibitions.

Membership is open to all students enrolled in the department, and leadership positions provide valuable opportunities to develop organizational and management skills.

When: Weekly meetings during term time

Where: Department Common Room

Membership: Open to all department students

International Conference Participation

The department provides financial support and guidance for students to participate in international conferences related to mathematics and computer science. Selected students, particularly those pursuing postgraduate studies, receive travel grants to present their research at prestigious conferences around the world.

This initiative exposes students to global research communities, diverse perspectives, and cutting-edge advancements in their fields of interest. Upon returning, participants share their experiences with peers through departmental seminars, extending the benefits to the wider student community.

The department maintains partnerships with several international universities, facilitating student exchanges and collaborative research opportunities.

Application Process: Submit research abstract and conference details

Eligibility: Priority given to postgraduate students with accepted papers

Support: Travel grants, registration fee coverage, presentation coaching
